About PhishGuard

An explainable phishing email risk analyzer built for transparency, privacy, and cybersecurity awareness.

✅ Fully Local 🔒 Privacy-First 🧠 Explainable AI 📧 Gmail Integration

🎯 What is PhishGuard?

PhishGuard is a rule-based, explainable phishing detection system that integrates directly with your Gmail account via secure OAuth 2.0. Unlike traditional black-box machine learning detectors, PhishGuard provides complete transparency — every decision is explained, every score is justified, and every finding is backed by evidence extracted from the email itself.

⚙️ The 5-Point Detection Engine

PhishGuard analyzes every email through five independent, specialized analyzers — each contributing to a transparent, additive risk score out of 100.

🔤 Max 25 pts

Keyword Analyzer

Detects known phishing phrases like "verify your account", "account suspended", or "click here immediately".

🔗 Max 25 pts

URL Analyzer

Flags shortened links, IP-based URLs, excessive subdomains, and mismatched anchor text.

👤 Max 20 pts

Sender Analyzer

Identifies brand impersonation (e.g., "PayPal" from an unrelated domain) and corporate claims from free email providers.

⏰ Max 15 pts

Urgency Analyzer

Detects psychological pressure tactics: urgency phrases, excessive CAPS, and alarming punctuation patterns.

📝 Max 15 pts

Structure Analyzer

Evaluates email structure — generic greetings, credential requests, and minimal body content.

QR Code Detection

📈

User Risk Profiling

Tracks your personal risk trends over time in a privacy-preserving way to help you improve your security posture.

🔍

Campaign Detection

Fingerprints analyzed emails to detect coordinated phishing campaigns — groups similar messages to reveal attacker patterns.

💬

AI Security Chatbot

Powered by Gemini AI, the built-in assistant answers security questions and explains findings in context with PII redaction.

🎯

Training Simulator

🔬

Ascend Research Framework

An advanced analytical layer providing deep interpretability, psychological profiling, and autonomous threat research.

📊 Scoring & Classification

Low Risk 0 – 33

Email appears legitimate with few or no suspicious indicators.

Medium Risk 34 – 66

Email shows some suspicious patterns — review with caution.

High Risk 67 – 100

Strong phishing indicators detected — exercise extreme caution.
